Aviation · 5 Airlines · Venezuela & LATAM

Where it started — regulated, high-stakes, and real

My career began in hangars and operations rooms across Venezuela and LATAM. At Aeropostal, I built and led the training center in Caracas — then my work expanded to Venezolana, Estelar, Avior, and a fifth carrier because my track record as a certified designer and facilitator preceded me.

The secret to scaling wasn't just the systems — it was making highly technical content processable. I combined whole-brain methodology and hemispheric activation with Bloom's Taxonomy so that Dangerous Goods protocols, Safety Management System (SMS) requirements, and Human Factors training didn't feel like heavy manuals. People laughed, practiced, and genuinely learned. SMEs brought the expertise. I made it learnable.

I designed programs approved by the Venezuelan Civil Aviation Authority — from Dangerous Goods and SMS to Crew Resource Management (CRM), Ramp Operations, and Recurrent Training. I led the IATA-certified C-suite program and collaborated with Lufthansa Consulting Group to elevate operational and commercial performance standards.

  • Coordinated Caracas training center — full ADDIE lifecycle from analysis to Proficiency Check
  • Designed and delivered Cabin Crew Advancement programs and pilot progression tracks (First Officer → Captain)
  • Certified 100+ facilitators across ground ops, flight, safety, maintenance, and customer service
  • Collaborated with Lufthansa Consulting Group on commercial performance improvement
  • Certified national and international stations in service quality — 2,000+ employees across 5 airlines

04
Consulting · Pharma Client (Confidential)

Turning a complex CRM into a tool reps actually trust

"Not a software training. A behavior-change program designed around real sales moments."

82%Real-time usage
40%Pipeline visibility
70%Habit consistency
3moTo results
Behavior-Driven DesignMicrolearningMobile-FirstDynamics 365

Sales reps who work with high autonomy don't respond well to being told to change how they work. When a large pharma client rolled out Dynamics 365, adoption stalled. The technology wasn't the problem — the experience of adopting it was.

I designed a behavior-driven learning experience built around real sales moments: how reps prioritize their day, manage their pipeline, stay consistent in the field. Microlearning in the flow of work. Scenario-based design grounded in actual customer situations. Visual storytelling that made complexity feel intuitive. Mobile-first so reps could learn wherever the day took them.

  • 82% of reps shifted to real-time Dynamics 365 usage — away from after-the-fact updates
  • Data accuracy and pipeline visibility improved by 40% in 3 months
  • 70% CRM habit consistency — reps using the system to prioritize and decide, not just log
  • Behavior-first design: scenario-based, mobile-first, grounded in real sales situations

El Vuelo de la Cometa — The Flight of the Kite — is an emotional intelligence and leadership program for adolescents aged 13–17. I designed everything from zero: curriculum, digital platform, facilitator certification model, blended learning structure, all materials in both English and Spanish.

The scale is real. Guatemala, Nicaragua, Colombia, Venezuela, and the United States. 3,000+ young people. 161 certified facilitators through a Train-the-Trainer model I architected. In 2025, a new partnership is bringing it to 1,000+ teens across five schools in Florida.

This program proves something I carry into every corporate role: when learning is designed for the whole human — mind, emotion, identity — it doesn't just educate. It transforms. And it scales.

  • Designed full bilingual curriculum, digital platform, and TTT certification from greenfield
  • Scaled to 5 countries with no degradation in quality — built for replication from day one
  • Measurable reductions in dropout rates and school violence in participating communities
  • 2025: 1,000 new teens in Florida — Acevedo Foundation partnership

How I think

Effective learning happens when we connect both the mind and the heart.

My cornerstones: consult before designing, identify root causes, influence without authority, create for behavior change — and build things that scale without me.

I

Consult before I design

I identify root causes before recommending solutions. Training is rarely the answer to every problem — and I know the difference.

II

Influence without authority

I've earned buy-in from aviation executives and pharma sales leaders. The approach: speak their language, show their results.

III

Design for behavior, not knowledge

What people know and what they do are different things. I design for the second one — with scenarios, simulations, and coaching loops.

IV

Measure what actually matters

Adoption. Behavior change. Time-to-proficiency. Commercial impact. Not completion rates.

V

Build to scale from day one

Every system I design — LMS governance, TTT models, content architecture — is built so others can replicate it without me in the room.

VI

Emotion drives memory

Neuroscience isn't a buzzword — it's how I design. People laugh, practice, and move through every level of Bloom's Taxonomy. That's intentional.
